Cardiovascular Disease
Risk Probability Calculator

A statistical screening tool developed from nationally representative U.S. survey data, designed to estimate the probability of cardiovascular disease in young adults aged 20–40 years based on dietary, clinical, and demographic factors.

❤️What is Cardiovascular Disease (CVD)?

For the purposes of this calculator, Cardiovascular Disease (CVD) is defined as a self-reported history of any of the following conditions, consistent with the NHANES survey criteria used in the underlying research model:

❤️ Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction) 🧠 Stroke ⚡ Angina Pectoris 💔 Congestive Heart Failure 🩸 Coronary Artery Disease 🫀 Other Heart Disease
⚠️

Important Notice: This tool provides a statistical probability estimate only and does not constitute a medical diagnosis. Results should not replace consultation with a qualified healthcare professional. Always seek the advice of your physician or other qualified health provider regarding any medical condition.

📋 Important Limitations

This probability estimate is derived from a cross-sectional binary logistic regression model fitted to NHANES 2011–2018 data among U.S. adults aged 20–40 years (n=6,902; CVD cases=101). The model has a c-statistic of 0.753 indicating acceptable discrimination. This is not a clinical diagnosis. The model does not account for family history of CVD, blood pressure, physical activity, LDL/HDL cholesterol levels, ECG findings, or other clinical measurements. Quasi-complete separation was detected in the model due to the rare outcome (1.3% prevalence), which may cause some estimates to be imprecise. All results should be discussed with a licensed healthcare professional. This tool is intended for educational and research purposes only.
